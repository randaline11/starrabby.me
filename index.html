How To Turn a Computer Scientist Into a Frog in 16 Easy Steps.

I have been called Ada Lovelace, fighting

insanity, poetical science--

Byron's number cruncher and note-taker.

I don't know why anyone would say that.

I crunch nothing. I take notes on old scraps.

I'm book drunk and stumbling in binary.

When I sit in the garden on Charles Street,

I don't see panoramas of numbers.

I see flowers. Don't most people? I'm not

screwing with you. I'm a calculus wimp.

But three years ago, I learned I transform.

When the full moon's out, my skin turns greener

than grass, my eyes protrude, and I grow bumps.

I crawl out from long-limbed Herons' long necks.

My alter ego isn't quite human,

but I can choke the oceans I grew in.

-Abby Starr, responding to a prompt by poet and professor Matthew Olzmann
